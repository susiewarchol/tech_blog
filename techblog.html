Blown to Bits

By Susie

Smile When You Snap

There are webcams everywhere and anyone can be recorded. If you commit a crime, someone can easily take photo or video evidence to prove you did it. Photos can also be traced back to people and find out things like the type of camera used to take the photo or the credit card used to purchase it.

Saving Money

Loyalty cards track what you purchase at a certain store. The customer gets coupons and ads based on what they buy to save money and in return, the store company knows what you buy. A privacy tax can be payed so that the store does not track your purchases.

How Sites Know Who You Are

Sites track everything you do using cookies and your IP address. You tell them who you are by logging into a mail account or a shopping website like amazon. When you buy something online, the site knows your name, address, and credit card information.

Reflection

Everything we do is public. It definetely is weird how much the internet knows about us, but a lot of this is done for good (such as security cameras catching criminals). Maybe my phone shouldn't be able to listen to each conversation I have, but it may not matter if everything about myself is already online.

Interview

By Susie

I am interviewing my mom who grew up in Europe and later moved to the United States.

Susie : How old were you when you got your first computer?

Mom : 20 years old.

Susie : What was your favorite piece of tecnhology when you were growing up?

Mom : My computer.

Susie : How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Mom : It has become easier to stay connected with people.

Susie : Have the changes you've seen in technology mostly helped or hurt society?

Mom : I would say help because it serves as a means of helping people stay close and connected.

Susie : What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Mom : I hope that communication will improve.

Susie : What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Mom : I am worried that we will become too dependent on it.

Susie : What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Mom : Kids these days relay too much on technology and are always on their phones.

Susie : What technology that exists today do you wish you had when you were younger?

Mom : The iPhone Xs Max.
